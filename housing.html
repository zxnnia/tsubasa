Alderman Hall-style Dorms

Also known as "new dorms", these are the most recently built (not renovated) dorms for first-years on grounds. Residents live in doubles, meaning two residents per room, in sex-segregated floors, where there is a common bathroom for all the residents of a floor.

Advantages of new dorms:

  • Closest to O-Hill Dining Hall (best breakfast on grounds) and Slaughter Gym
  • Up to six stories high depending on the building, if you get a high floor you will probably have a nice view from your window
  • Most modern-looking from the outside
  • Study areas on each floor
    •

    Disadvantages of new dorms:

  • Difference in interior quality varies heavily depending on which building
  • Ugly interior design for the hallways
  • Hall-style bathrooms and resultant lack of in-room amenities
    •

    I mentioned each building is different. There are a total of nine different buildings in new dorms, which all have their advantages and disadvantages.
